Biography

I work as an assistant professor of Remote Sensing and Image Processing at the Department of Earth and Space Sciences. My research interests include developing algorithms and methodologies usinf machine learning techniques for processing very high resolution remote sensing images for natural and man-made resource management. I have more than 15 years of experience in handing and processing geospatial images from multispectral/ hyperspectral/ LiDAR sensors captured from various platforms such as satellite, airborne, drone/UAV, ground-based and mobile platforms.

Of particular interest, lately, I focus on data from LiDAR Remote sensing technology, which is one of the most unprecedented geospatial for direct and accurate measurement of elevation of the earth surface leading to 3D mapping of earth surface features. Due to the unstructured nature of the data and its high volume, localizing and extracting various urban objects in 3D perspective from the LiDAR data, remains a challenge.This inherent nature of the point cloud poses several exciting research areas I have been working on developing algorithms and methodologies related to automated processing of LiDAR point cloud for various applications
